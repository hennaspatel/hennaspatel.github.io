In the numbers

Globally, internet voting in elections is not a picture of modernity. But we are only on the precipice of applying technlogical innovation to this fundamental democratic right.  

24

Less than 24 countries

have used internet voting or electronic voting in a binding political election. This number includes elections where ballots were cast using; a remote personal device (internet voting), at a centralalized electronic voting machine/station/kiosk (EVMs), or on paperâ€” but counted electronically. Many of these countries abandoned i-voting and e-voting upon experiencing issues of security, fraud, miscounting, and compromised secrecy of the ballot.

Issues & advantages of internet voting.

Among the issues cited when a country abandons or has no plans to implement - these 6: Security & ballot secrecy, poor internet access & the digital divide, fraud & coercion, and vote buying & miscounting.

6 top advantages from proponents of internet voting: Potential for greater voter turnout. Drawing younger voters. Ease of use. Few to no ballot errors.  Faster election results. And lower adminstration costs.
